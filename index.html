Less Than News

Just a bunch of college students trying to say what we think is worth saying. Whatever literature you do find here, it isn’t yet news, not quite, but it does possess the beginnings of it. And it is in those beginnings that we hope to someday find the becoming’s of it, as well. Regardless of whether or not we’ll be any more qualified to declare them so then than we are now, & regardless of whether or not either of us has any business trying to find out. We might not, on both counts. But then again, what is youth if not fighting a war you did not start.

Journalism, innit?

Founders’ Note,

It is shameful to admit that, despite being one of only seven colleges in the University of Delhi that prides itself on having a department of ‘Journalism’ - regardless of how poorly administered or funded - Kalindi College, the google maps approved ‘hidden gem’ of ‘North Campus’, does not have its own nor is affiliated with any original college news publication or journal. Which isn’t to say that it has much else big named to offer, really. It’s not like it’s a pity party for Journalism specifically. To be clear.

But anyway. An absence is apparent. Or so it became to us at some point after the start of TVE as an Intro to New Media project, a means to an end, a most honorable end of scraping by a grand total of 10% of our grade. Institution & authority, in their arms the swords of bureaucracy & status quo. There is an impossibility to capturing a certain elusive creature called progress when faced with these two goons, particularly if you are but subordinate parts of these very institutions. Ants to a god, cog in the machine. News cannot be published this way, & no one else seems to want to publish it any which way at all. An independent publishing, run by the microscopic cogs & for the microscopic cogs, becomes necessary.

In October, 2007, a small independent student run newspaper called DU Beat began to fill part of this absence. And it has done a brilliant job of doing so, till date, perhaps now having reached the upper bound of its expansion. But in its expansion, DU Beat has now itself turned into an institution, defeating the purpose of its ‘independence’ in the first place. Which isn’t to mention that in its significance, it’s publishings also follow a naturally proportionate order of that which may be deemed significant. Trends, universities, big named colleges, lighthearted vanity. Macroscopic cogs, if you will. And then in June last year, with RK Jha’s guest lecture at Hindu ushering in a new era of non-independent student publishing, & with the Hindu College Gazette far exceeding in subject & in quality what one imagined college zines to be capable of, some more of the absence was filled. But again, an institutional publishing has institutional hurdles. There must (might/should/hopefully will) be more publishings here & there. But if it is possible for you & I to be so effortlessly oblivious to their existences then it only makes sense why the hole feels still so desperately gaping & unfilled.

Are several small phalluses better than a single one of behemothian capacities? Debatable. But the point remains, there is at least one more phallus, of indetermined & possibly minimal size, required still. That is what we hope to become. And the burden of that hope, is the legacy we’ll leave behind, in the hands of our successors at the department, along with whomsoever wishes to be part. These things need to keep moving. We hope this one goes far.
